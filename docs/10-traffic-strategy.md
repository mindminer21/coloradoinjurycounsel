## 0. The strategic frame (why this works now)

The modern acquisition playbook (Hormozi and the broader direct-response school) points at one asset stack — and we already built most of it:

| Framework concept | Our implementation |
|---|---|
| Lead magnet (a complete solution to one narrow problem) | Case-value estimator (diagnostic type) + Sky's instant phone intake |
| Intent-matched bridge pages | 100 intent-matched landing pages, each a pre-frame bridge |
| Traffic you OWN | HighLevel list, tagged + attributed at the source |
| Follow-up funnel | HighLevel sequences (to build — Phase 1 below) |
| Value ladder | Estimator → consultation → representation (contingency) |

**The market timing thesis (from docs/01):** SB26-174 makes third-party lead-selling a deceptive trade practice (~Aug 2026). Competitors who rent their pipeline from lead brokers lose supply. Every dollar below builds pipeline **we own**. Hard rule for this entire document: **no lead marketplaces, no per-lead purchases from third-party generators, no paid referral arrangements** (also barred by Colo. RPC 7.2). Firm-branded advertising only.

**Honesty adaptations (important):** These frameworks assume an info-product seller's freedom. A law firm cannot use manufactured scarcity, fake urgency, outcome promises, or "results-based certainty" claims. Every tactic below is filtered through Colo. RPC 7.1–7.3. Where a book tactic can't be used honestly, it's adapted or dropped and marked ⚖️.

## 1. Unit economics — what "profitable" means here (do this math first)

The governing metric (per Hormozi): **LTGP:CAC ≥ 3:1** — lifetime gross profit at least 3× the cost to acquire. For contingency PI (assumptions to validate against firm data):

- Blended gross fee per signed case (mix of soft-tissue and serious): assume **$10,000–$15,000** conservative blended; serious cases far higher. Use **$12,000** for planning.
- Therefore **maximum allowable CAC per signed case ≈ $4,000** (3:1 floor).
- Typical funnel rates to plan against (industry-reasonable, must be measured): qualified lead → consultation ≈ 50–60% (Sky booking live raises this), consultation → signed ≈ 25–40%. So ≈ **6–8 qualified leads per signed case** → **target blended cost per qualified lead (CPL): ≤ $500**, with organic channels pulling the blend far down.
- **Cash-flow caveat (client-financed acquisition doesn't apply):** contingency fees arrive months/years after spend. Paid budget must be sized as patient capital — set a monthly ceiling you can carry for 12–18 months without revenue from it. This cash-timing reality is the single biggest adaptation required for contingency practice, and the reason organic-first sequencing matters.
- Every channel below gets measured on **cost per *signed case***, not per click or call. The attribution rails exist: UTMs + source page + keyword flow into HighLevel on every estimator submission and Sky call.

## 2. The offer layer — fix this before buying traffic

Traffic multiplies the offer; it doesn't fix it (Hormozi's first law). "Free consultation" is what every competitor says. Rebuild the two front-end offers around the value drivers Hormozi popularized (↑dream outcome, ↑perceived likelihood, ↓time, ↓effort):

**2a. The lead magnet (exists — sharpen the wrapper).** Rename the estimator with reason-why naming, honestly: **"The Colorado Case Value Snapshot — a free 2-minute educational read on what drives cases like yours, under Colorado's new 2025 damages law."** Reason-why (2025 law change) + audience (Colorado) + outcome (know what drives value) + time (2 minutes) + a concrete container word (Snapshot). ⚖️ No manufactured download caps — scarcity is dropped for the magnet.

**2b. The consultation (rename + stack).** Not "free consultation" but the **"Colorado Claim Game Plan Session"** — 30 minutes, and the caller/visitor is told exactly what they leave with, each named (build once, deliver to every prospect):
1. A deadline check for their specific claim type (urgency that is *real* in PI — statutes of limitation — honest urgency, not manufactured)
2. An evidence-preservation checklist for their incident type (from our content system)
3. A straight answer on whether the case justifies counsel — "if you'd do fine on your own, we'll say so" (differentiator; already site copy)
4. The insurance-conversation briefing (what recorded statements do)
- **Guarantee (the strongest lever, and law-native):** the contingency fee IS the implied guarantee — *"You pay no fee unless we recover for you."* Lead with it everywhere (⚖️ pending attorney confirmation of engagement practice + required disclaimers about costs vs. fees).
- ⚖️ Dropped from the standard playbook: bonus stacking with dollar values, cohort scarcity, expiring pricing — inappropriate for legal services.

**2c. The one core belief.** One belief, repeated everywhere, that topples the rest: **"Insurers pay full value only to claimants they believe are prepared to go to trial — and preparation is exactly what a national trial platform provides from day one."** Every ad, page, email, and Sky's framing already align with this. Supporting false-belief content list (each gets a story-driven piece, not a lecture): "my case is too small to bother a lawyer" · "lawyers take most of the money" · "the insurer's first offer is close to fair" · "hiring a lawyer means suing and courtrooms" · "it's too late for me" · "all injury firms are billboard mills."

**2d. The authority persona / movement.** The authority persona is the *team story*: Jeff as the Denver-based leader (backstory: Colorado attorney who built the firm's Mountain West front door), Masten as the reformed insider — **"he spent years defending insurers; now he uses their playbook for injured people."** That conversion-story arc is our single best narrative asset. Movement slogan (campaign-sign simple): **"Coloradans shouldn't get discount justice."** ⚖️ All client-story content requires real, consented, non-identifying examples or none at all — no invented parables presented as real.

## 3. Funnel architecture — mapped to what's live

**Traffic temperature → page mapping** (already built, now explicit policy):
- **Hot** (searching our name / repeat visitors): homepage, About, direct estimator. Short bridge.
- **Warm** (searching the problem: "denver car accident lawyer"): the 100 landing pages ARE the pre-frame bridge layer — educate → estimator/call. This is why they're content-rich, not thin doorways.
- **Cold** (interrupted on social/YouTube): NEVER send to the homepage. Send to dedicated long-bridge pages (to build): story-first structure, then the estimator. Phase-2 build: 3 cold bridge pages (car crash / serious injury / wrongful death).

**The follow-up funnel (biggest current gap — Phase 1 priority).** ~81% of conversions happen at contact 5+. Estimator and Sky both capture email + consent; today nothing systematic follows. Build in HighLevel:
1. **Story-driven intro sequence** (5 emails, days 0–5): (1) here's your snapshot + what it can't tell you → (2) Masten's insider story → (3) the epiphany: preparation changes outcomes + Game Plan Session offer → (4) hidden factors (deadlines, evidence decay, comparative fault) → (5) honest urgency: *your* deadline clock is real → book/call CTA. ⚖️ Every email: attorney-advertising footer, unsubscribe, no outcome promises.
2. **Ongoing weekly cadence** (weekly, not daily — legal-appropriate): one story or Colorado-law development per week, each tying to the Session offer. Doubles as the content engine's email leg.
3. **Multichannel**: email + (with captured consent) SMS reminder for booked consultations + site-visitor retargeting audiences (§5).
4. **Speed-to-lead**: estimator submissions with phone numbers get a Sky **outbound** follow-up call within 5 minutes during business hours (Retell outbound agent is already bound to the number). ⚖️ Outbound only to people who submitted contact info with consent — never cold calls, never solicitation of known accident victims.

## 4. Organic engines (Hormozi's four channels, adapted, with single-platform discipline)

**4a. Owned search (already 80% built — finish the last mile).** Submit sitemap to GSC (docs/07 steps); buy the brand domain (docs/02); build the backlink base with the free/legitimate layer: state bar directory, Avvo/Justia/FindLaw *profile listings* (⚖️ profiles are fine; their paid lead-gen products are not), legal + local directories, Google Business Profile for the Denver office (reviews engine: post-matter review requests via HighLevel), local citations with the consistent new NAP. Monthly content cadence afterward: 4 new pieces/month aimed at cluster gaps GSC reveals (More → Better → New: expand what ranks before adding new clusters).

**4b. Content engine (over-give, ask rarely; one platform mastered first).** Legal demand is search-based, so the platform order is **YouTube → (repurpose) → everything else** — meet people where they already search:
- **The master show:** one 10–15 min weekly video, Jeff (or Jeff+guest) on one false belief or one Colorado-law reality. Hook → story → offer structure; teach *what and why*, sell the *how* (the Session). Titles = our value-cluster keywords ("What's a Colorado car-accident case actually worth in 2026?").
- Chop each into: 3 Shorts/Reels, 1 landing-page embed (the matching cluster page gets the video — SEO dwell bonus), 1 weekly email, quote cards for GBP posts.
- Give:ask ratio ≥ 3.5:1. CTA is always the Snapshot or the Session, never "hire us."
- Volume discipline, adapted: **100 minutes/day** of content production/distribution effort across the team, 100 days, before judging results.

**4c. The Colorado Target-100 (earned media only).** Build the list of existing congregations of injured-or-adjacent Coloradans: Denver/CO Springs TV consumer-affairs segments, Colorado Sun/Denverite/Westword reporters on traffic-safety beats, CO personal-finance and outdoor-recreation podcasts, ski-community newsletters and forums, cycling clubs, neighborhood Facebook/Nextdoor groups, chiropractors/PT clinics/body shops/tow operators (relationship + education only — ⚖️ **nothing of value exchanged for referrals**, RPC 7.2), CU/DU law-media, HOA newsletters. Relationship-first protocol: follow, engage, serve for 4–6 weeks before any ask; the "ask" is always educational (interview Jeff about the 2025 damages-cap change — genuinely newsworthy), never "send us clients." Expect ~10% to convert into earned placements; each placement gets retargeted (§5) so no visit is wasted.

**4d. Warm outreach (week 1, costs nothing).** One personal announcement from Jeff to his full Colorado network (email + LinkedIn): the firm now handles injury matters statewide, here's the 2-minute Snapshot to share with anyone who gets hurt. ⚖️ To acquaintances as announcement — not targeted solicitation of anyone known to be injured. Repeat quarterly with a story, not an ask.

## 5. Paid engines — profitable and compliant (sequenced last, per Hormozi, but planned now)

**Sequencing rule (Hormozi's ordering):** paid comes last and scales what organic proves. Launch order below; each phase must hit its gate before the next opens. All ads are firm-branded, land on our own pages, and comply with RPC 7.1 (no outcome promises, no "best," dramatization labeled).

**5a. Google Search PPC (first paid dollar — highest intent, provable).**
- Structure: exact/phrase campaigns mirroring our top-opportunity keyword clusters (docs/05). Start with the 15 value-cluster + Denver head terms. Ads land on the *matching* landing page (message match = quality score = cheaper clicks).
- PI reality: Denver head-term CPCs run $80–250+. The profitable wedge is **long-tail + educational angle**: "what is my case worth"–type queries cost a fraction and feed the Snapshot. Two campaign layers: (1) high-intent "lawyer/attorney" terms with call-focused ads (Sky answers 24/7 — a genuine conversion advantage over competitors' voicemail after 5pm), (2) value-intent terms driving the Snapshot at low CPC.
- **Testing budget rule (adapted from Hormozi):** kill a keyword/ad after spending 2× the target CPL with zero qualified leads; 10× budget behind winners. Start: **$3–5k/month**, gate to scale: blended paid CPL ≤ $500 and ≥1 signed case attributable within 90 days.
- Branded defense campaign ("whiteford injury") from day one — cheap insurance.

**5b. Google Local Services Ads (⚖️ attorney review required first).** LSAs are Google's pay-per-lead product with "Google Screened" badging. Economically excellent for PI, **but** SB26-174 targets paying for leads — whether a platform's own ad format under the firm's name falls in scope is a legal question Jeff must answer before activation. Documented here; not activated.

**5c. Meta (Facebook/Instagram) — retargeting first, cold second.**
- **Retargeting (launch with 5a):** site visitors + estimator starters who didn't finish + video viewers. Small budget ($500–1,000/mo), high efficiency; creative = the false-belief series and the courtroom film cut (label: dramatization). Frequency caps + sensitivity: injury audiences get educational tone, never "were you injured?" callouts (⚖️ avoids solicitation optics and Meta policy friction).
- **Cold interruption (only after 4b produces proven hooks):** hook-story-offer creative tested organically first; CO-geo, broad targeting; goal = Snapshot completions, not clicks. Gate: paid CPL from Meta ≤ 2× Google's before scaling.
- **Target-100 leverage:** run ads to followers/engagers of the Colorado congregations from 4c where targeting allows.

**5d. YouTube pre-roll (after 4b's channel exists):** :15/:30 cuts of the master show + film, targeting CO + in-market/legal-services + crash-related search behavior. Cheap CPMs, feeds retargeting pools, builds the omnipresence effect for everyone who touched the site.

**5e. Explicitly excluded:** third-party lead marketplaces, per-lead brokers, "partner" firms selling cases, paid referral deals, review-buying, billboard/TV (revisit at >$50k/mo digital spend). Radio/OTT: later, only with dedicated tracking numbers.

**Budget arc (patient capital, monthly):** Months 1–2: $0 paid (organic + follow-up funnel build). Months 3–4: $4–6k (5a + retargeting). Months 5–6: $8–12k if gates pass. Month 7+: scale winners toward the $4k/signed-case ceiling, in strict order: more budget on winners → fix the worst funnel step → only then new channels.

- **Weeks 1–2:** GSC submission · GBP setup + review engine · warm-outreach announcement · story-driven intro sequence live in HighLevel · Sky outbound speed-to-lead on · brand domain decision.
